How to Install the Cinestyle Preset on Canon Cameras: A Step-by-Step Guide

Posted on Mar 8, 2023 DRAFT

Cinestyle is a popular camera preset that is widely used in the film and video production industry. It is a picture style that is designed to give Canon cameras a flatter and more neutral color profile. This allows for greater flexibility in post-production, giving filmmakers more control over the final look of their footage. Installing the Cinestyle preset on a Canon camera is a relatively simple process that can be completed in a few easy steps.

Before we dive into the installation process, let’s briefly discuss what Cinestyle is and why it is so popular. Cinestyle was created by Technicolor, a company that specializes in post-production services for the film and video industry. The preset is designed to create a flatter and more neutral color profile that retains more detail in the shadows and highlights. This makes it easier to color grade footage in post-production, giving filmmakers more control over the final look of their film or video.

To install the Cinestyle preset on a Canon camera, you will need the following:

A Canon DSLR or mirrorless camera A computer with internet access An SD card or CF card (depending on your camera model) A USB cable to connect your camera to your computer Here are the steps to install the Cinestyle preset on your Canon camera:

Step 1: Download the Cinestyle preset

The first step is to download the Cinestyle preset from the Technicolor website (https://www.technicolor.com/cinestyle). You can find the download link by searching for “Cinestyle download” on Google or by visiting the Technicolor website directly. Once you have downloaded the preset, save it to your computer.

Step 2: Transfer the preset to your camera

Next, you need to transfer the Cinestyle preset to your camera. To do this, insert your SD card or CF card into your camera and connect it to your computer using the USB cable. Your camera should appear as a removable drive on your computer. Navigate to the root directory of your camera’s memory card and create a new folder called “CANON”. Inside the CANON folder, create another folder called “MISC”. Copy the Cinestyle preset file to the MISC folder.

Step 3: Install the preset on your camera

Once you have transferred the Cinestyle preset to your camera, it is time to install it. To do this, turn on your camera and navigate to the Picture Style menu. Select User Def. 1 or User Def. 2, depending on which one is empty, and then press the Set button. Scroll down to “Load Custom Picture Style” and press the Set button again. Select “OK” when prompted to load the custom picture style. The Cinestyle preset should now be installed on your camera.

Step 4: Start shooting with Cinestyle

Congratulations bRo, you have successfully installed the Cinestyle preset on your Canon camera! Now it’s time to start shooting with it. Keep in mind that Cinestyle is designed to be used in conjunction with color grading in post-production. This means that your footage may look flat and desaturated when you view it on your camera’s LCD screen. However, don’t let this discourage you. The beauty of Cinestyle is that it gives you more control over the final look of your footage in post-production.
